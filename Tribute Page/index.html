Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

"You can't build peace on empty stomachs" ~ Dr. Norman Borlaug.

Here is a Timeline of Dr. Norman Borlaug Life:

  • March 25, 1914: Norman Ernest Borlaug is born in Cresco, Iowa, United States.
  • 1933: Borlaug enrolls at the University of Minnesota, where he studies forestry.
  • 1935: He switches his major to plant pathology and genetics, focusing on agriculture.
  • 1942: Borlaug earns his Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota.
  • 1944: He joins the Cooperative Wheat Research and Production Program in Mexico, working to improve wheat production.
  • 1950s: Borlaug's research leads to the development of high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties, which play a crucial role in the Green Revolution.
  • 1964: He is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to increasing food production and alleviating world hunger.
  • 1966: Borlaug establishes the International Maize and Wheat Improvement Center (CIMMYT) in Mexico to further his research and promote agricultural development.
  • 1970: He receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom, one of the highest civilian awards in the United States.
  • 1984: Borlaug founds the World Food Prize, an annual international award recognizing individuals who have made significant contributions to improving global food security.
  • 2006: The United States Congress honors Norman Borlaug with a Congressional Gold Medal for his lifetime achievements.
  • September 12, 2009: Dr. Norman Borlaug passes away at the age of 95 in Dallas, Texas, leaving behind a lasting legacy in agriculture and humanitarian efforts.
